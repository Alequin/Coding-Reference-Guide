What is the Software Development Life Cycle

The Software Development Life Cycle (sdlc) or software development process describes the distinct work phases and methods used to plan, design, develop and test software.

The sdlc is a process used by those who create software to plan, develop maintain, replace, alter and enhance software. Common stages of a sdlc might include...

  • Planning: Planning (or requirement analysis) is the most important stage. Input for multiple sources is used to outline the basic project approach and conduct product feasibility studies. Plans should also consider the quality assurance requirements and identify possible risks.
  • Defining: Projects requirements must be clearly defined and documented in a software requirement specification (SRS) document.
  • Designing: Using the SRS the product architecture is defined. More than one approach may be proposed and documented in a Designing Document Specification (DDS).
  • Building: In this stage the product is built.
  • Testing: The product is tested for defects and bugs and fixed until it reaches the desired quality. This stage is commonly a subset within other stages is most modern sdlc models.
  • Deployment: Once complete the product is released
